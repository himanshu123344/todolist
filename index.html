Frequently Asked Questions

What is FSC Certification?

FSC Certification stands for Forest Stewardship Council Certification. It is a globally recognized certification system that ensures responsible management of forests and the products derived from them.

Why is FSC Certification important?

FSC Certification ensures that forests are managed in an environmentally responsible, socially beneficial, and economically viable manner. It helps to protect biodiversity, support local communities, and combat deforestation.

How does FSC Certification work?

FSC Certification is granted to companies that meet the FSC's rigorous standards for responsible forest management. This involves compliance with ecological, social, and economic criteria, including the protection of wildlife, indigenous rights, and the prohibition of illegal logging.

Who can get FSC Certified?

Any forest management organization or company involved in the production or trade of forest products can seek FSC Certification. This includes forest owners, logging companies, manufacturers, retailers, and traders.
